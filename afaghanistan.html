Providing quality healthcare services to the people of Afghanistan.

Managing health systems through public-private partnerships

In Afghanistan, the Aga Khan Development Network (AKDN) has been working in partnership with the government to manage health systems through public-private partnerships. The AKDN has been involved in the development and management of several healthcare facilities across the country, including hospitals, clinics, and health centers. These partnerships have helped to improve access to healthcare services for the Afghan population, particularly in remote and underserved areas. The AKDN's involvement in healthcare management has also contributed to capacity building and training for healthcare professionals in Afghanistan.

Message from Dr. Nasrullah Orya
Chief Executive Officer, AKHS, Afghanistan

As the nation suffers more tragedies of gender inequality and poverty, we must focus on both, short-term and long-term solutions that energize behavior into action, and drive efforts to advance diversity, equity and inclusion to help us rebuild our health system for the better.

AKHS, Afghanistan works in partnership with international donors and the Ministry of Public Health through the Aga Khan Development Network (AKDN) and the Aga Khan Foundation (AKF) to invest financial resources, as well as human capital to build back the local healthcare system, as well as deploy training and medical education programmes for healthcare professionals in the country. By lending our expertise and experience, we strive to create and operate sustainable health systems that provide top-tier care to the people of Afghanistan regardless of where they live.

History & Milestones

AKHS, A has a rich history of partnering with international donors, as well as local health system stakeholders to help rebuild the country's health infrastructure. In addition to leveraging our expertise and experience to operate health institutions, we have worked collaboratively to improve training deployed to healthcare workers in the region and have worked alongside the government to bring the right human and financial resources to the forefront to expand the quality and depth of care offered to the people of Afghanistan.
